# Copywriting — Voice, Tone, Trilingual Texture

The site's copy is inseparable from its design. Sterile marketing copy kills the aesthetic; poetic copy lifts it.

## Voice Attributes

- **Poetic, not promotional**: "each piece is crafted to gently brighten your mood" > "buy our candles"
- **Onomatopoeic**: Japanese mood-words (uki uki, doki doki, fuwari, pitter-patter) are load-bearing
- **Short lines**: enjambed like poetry — never long marketing paragraphs
- **Trilingual**: JA / EN / ZH coexist, each doing a different job
- **Humble**: "a tender touch" / "a moment to feel uplifted" — never superlatives

## Hero Headline Formula

Pattern:

```
Everyday <onomatopoeia>. Sometimes <onomatopoeia>.
```

The site's example: `Everyday uki uki. Sometimes doki doki.`

Why it works: one mood for the common day, one for the occasional spark. Two contrasting onomatopoeia. Parallel structure.

Alternative openings (same formula):
- `Everyday pitter-patter. Sometimes flutter-flutter.`
- `每天 kira kira. 偶尔 zawa zawa.` (if leaning ZH)
- `Mostly soft. Sometimes loud.`

**Anti-pattern**: "Welcome to our store." / "Where art meets craft." — do not write these.

## Brand-Intro Block (bilingual column)

Two columns. Left = poetic JA. Right = EN translation with *matched line breaks*. ZH optional.

Example from the site:

```
お気に入りの歌をハミングするように        | Like when humming your favorite tune,
思わずステップを踏んでいきたいように。      | Like when inadvertently moving to the rhythm.

気分がふわりと軽くなるように              | Each piece is crafted to
ひとつひとつ、仕上げていきました。         | gently brighten your mood.

ぬくもりの手触りと                        | A tender touch,
笑顔が込み上げる瞬間を。                  | a moment to feel uplifted.

こころが、やさしく弾むプロダクト。         | A product that
                                         | makes your heart flutter softly.
```

**Rules**:
- 3–5 stanzas, each 2–3 lines
- Match breath-points, not literal word order
- JA must read like poetry (use commas, not periods, between clauses)
- EN must not try to be literal — prioritize mood
- ZH version (if 3rd column) uses 4-character rhythms when possible

## Product Names — Single Kanji/Hanzi

Names that appear on product cards (on the site): `于 / 虹 / 芩 / 了 / 断 / 扽 / 裳 / 舞 / 果 / 色 / 常 / 在`

**Rules**:
- ONE character per product
- Prefer characters with evocative meaning (舞 = dance, 光 = light, 风 = wind)
- Prefer visual beauty over semantic clarity — the character is also an art object
- Don't use common brand words (品牌/系列/产品)

For non-CJK audiences: show the character at 16px + tiny English gloss below at 11px.

## Collection Titles — Evocative Phrases (serif italic)

Formula: `<modifier> <noun> [+ of/found/from <context>]`

Site examples:
- `Daily Flowers`
- `Stones & Gemstones Found on Imaginary Islands`
- `The Garden of Reverie`

**Rules**:
- 3–7 words
- Sensory or metaphorical, never functional
- Title Case, serif italic
- ZH tagline below in 1 sentence, 10–25 characters

## Section Labels — Single Capitalized Words

From the site: `Products`, `About`, `News`, `Contact`, `Collections Archives`

**Rules**:
- One capitalized English word in handwritten cursive
- Exception: `Collections Archives` = two words (paired with "Archives" to signal museum-quality)
- Never translate these to JA/ZH — the English is more brand-consistent

## News / Date-Indexed Items

Format:

```
YYYY.MM.DD    [Chinese Title]
              English Subtitle / Translation
              [tag1] [tag2]
              1-2 sentence Chinese excerpt ending with …
```

Site example:

```
2024.12.31    今日未来馆 AI 艺术特展 & 红树林 AI 艺术万人展
              Today's Future Hall AI Art Special Exhibition & Mangrove AI Art Exhibition of Ten Thousands
              [群展] [AI艺术]
              参与今日未来馆AI艺术特展及红树林AI艺术万人展，以AI艺术创作探索人工智能与当代艺术的交汇边界。…
```

## Contact Channel Labels

Use native-language terms for each channel:
- `微信` (not "WeChat" for ZH-speakers; WeChat is the OS term in English contexts)
- `X · TWITTER` (uppercase with middle dot — reads as curated label)
- `邮箱` (not "Email")
- `视频号` (WeChat Video platform — never translate)
- `小红书` / `Xiaohongshu`

**Rule**: labels in native terms, handles in monospace-like italic serif (`xiaoer-zhan`, `@xiaoerzhan`).

## Micro-copy

- `SCROLL` (uppercase, rotated 90°, small caps side label)
- `CHECK THE PRODUCTS!` (uppercase, rotated on opposite side)
- `Close ✕` (nav drawer close — uppercase word + unicode ✕)
- `New` / `仅剩少量` / `含附赠` (product badges — 1–3 char pills with rounded border)

## What NOT to Write

These lines would violate the aesthetic:

- ❌ "Shop our exclusive collection" (too commercial)
- ❌ "Free shipping on orders over $50" (not this kind of site)
- ❌ "Sign up for our newsletter!" (no exclamation-point marketing)
- ❌ "About Us" (use "About" only — "Us" is corporate)
- ❌ "Follow us on Instagram" (just show the handle with the platform label)

## One Overarching Principle

**The copy should read like the back cover of a poetry book**, not a product page. If a line sounds like it could appear on an Etsy store, rewrite it.
